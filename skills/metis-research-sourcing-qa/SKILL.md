# Métis Research Sourcing & QA

## Web Search Limitations
- **The Canadian Encyclopedia, Historica Canada, and academic journals block web_extract and web_search** (400 errors). 
- **Wikipedia pages also often block extraction.**
- **Solution**: Do NOT rely on live scraping for academic citations. Use the verified bibliography below. Use the browser tool for individual Wikipedia pages only to check their *References* section for leads, then verify those sources manually or use the known verified list.

## Non-Métis People Discovered in Metis Files (remove or reframe)
- **Marguerite "the Cree Woman"** — Cree wife of Cuthbert Grant Sr. Explicitly identified as Cree, not Metis.
- **Angelique Riel** — French-Canadian (daughter of Jean-Baptiste Lagimodiere and Marie-Anne Gaboury). First European woman in the West, NOT Metis.
- **Marie-Anne Gaboury** — French-Canadian, first European woman to settle in Western Canada. NOT Metis.
- General rule: Indigenous First Nations mothers/wives of fur traders were NOT Metis themselves. Their children were the first generation of Metis. Do NOT conflate.

## Verified Replacement Patterns
- **"Half-Breed Scholarship Fund" (unverifiable)** — Replace with verified Section 31 of Manitoba Act history: the 1.4 million acre land grant for Metis children, the scrip system substitution, speculator fraud, and how nominal educational provisions tied to scrip arrangements were grossly inadequate. Cite Sprague, Ens, Teillet.

## Peer Review Workflow
1. Write research files.
2. Run `delegate_task` with the `peer-review` skill or a comprehensive prompt.
3. **Critical Fixes:** Remove fabricated citations, remove Wikipedia from source lists, verify claims about specific people/funds.
4. **Structure Fixes:** Standardize frontmatter, add wikilinks, ensure TLDR in every file.
5. **Content Gaps:** Identify missing themes (Art, Kinship, Veterans, 2SLGBTQ+, Food).
6. Save the review to `99-Project-Management/PEER_REVIEW_[Topic].md`.

## Common Pitfalls
- **Never cite Wikipedia directly** in the vault. Use it to find authors/titles, then use the verified bibliography.
- **Never cite metismuseum.ca** as an academic source; it's a community archive. Use Morton/Denig/Peterson instead.
- **AI Hallucination Risk:** The model often invents plausible-sounding academic citations. Always cross-check against this list.
- **Web Extract Blocks:** Assume Canadian academic sites will block you. Use the verified list.
- **Non-Métis People:** Do not include non-Métis people in "Métis Leaders" lists (e.g., Marie-Anne Gaboury was not Métis).
